Head Socket — Round 1 (design selection)

📖 Round runbook →

The team-wide right-temple socket / gang mark, designed as a standalone asset. Stage-1 bake-off: 5 plate silhouettes × 3 engines (flash31 / gptimage2med / grokq1k), port construction fixed to the collared socket-hole. Verdicts are fresh-context crop audits; the human eye-call is open.

Pre-read. Every design has at least one clean roll to judge from. Cross-engine read: flash31 and gptimage2med are reliably on-brief; grokq1k is the weakest engine on every design (fragile collars, a 4-hole count error on P5, forehead misplacement on P2) — so judge each design on its strongest roll. P1/P4/P5/P6 each landed 2 clean keepers; P2 landed 1. The surprise: P6 (Jason's mecha-wolf) actually pulled off the wolf-AND-jack double-read on both strong engines.

Dimitri's preliminary read (needs Jason): the sockets look too big vs the heads and stand out too much from the surface. Note the sizing/relief in these renders is deliberately oversized insurance against Meshy reconstruction loss — but we've since chosen to deliver the socket as an authored STL fused per character, which removes that need entirely, so we're free to shrink the plate and lower the relief to taste in Stage 2 (only floors: reads at arm's length, features ≥0.6 mm). Judge the silhouettes here as glyphs; treat their current size/proudness as tunable, not fixed.
