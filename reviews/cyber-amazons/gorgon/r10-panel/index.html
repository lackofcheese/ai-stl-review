Gorgon — Round 10 (Meshy run)

📖 Round runbook →

Round 10 reconstructs Gorgon in 3D — Meshy multi-view on the Round-9 r2 clay sheet, two rolls (m1, m2) as the standard defect-lottery insurance. Meshes are scaled to 37 mm total height (team “32 mm heroic”), feet floored at z=0. Pick the cleaner roll for the live-3D print gate.

Pre-read. Both rolls reconstructed cleanly: single watertight-for-slicing shell (a clean 2-manifold except ~24–25 sub-mm self-touch pinch edges — slicer-repairable; 0 holes, winding-consistent), ~0.93–0.94 cm³, all identity kit present — open stiff-arm palm, trailing fist, the single asymmetric shoulder pad, braids over the pad-side shoulder, segmented spine, both knee pads, laced/buckled boots. No ghost props (she holds nothing), no doubled limbs, no floating parts; the thin-parts probe flagged no fragile necks. One systematic quirk on both rolls: a flat sole-plate reads under the trailing (mid-stride) boot — from the walk pose, not a per-roll defect. Recommendation: effectively a TIE, narrow lean to m1. m1 is cleaner on the hard-surface armor (pad, seams, spine — fewer non-manifold spikes; its only blemishes are a tiny fingertip hole + minor dreadlock gaps that auto-fill); m2 owns the hero feature — a crisper raised open palm with a clearer thumb and no fingertip hole, plus cleaner braids — but carries a wider ragged non-manifold fringe on the pad rim/shoulder seams that is more cleanup. Both are viable inputs; the call is close enough that it's your live-3D eye.

  3. r1 clay — leave dropped, or run it? (ADDRESSED in r11)Dimitri

    r1 was dropped this round because its clay blurs into the background — partly a crop-tool issue (the figure's low-contrast interior was painted over as background). The tooling was subsequently hardened on main (PR #80) with a non-destructive rectangular crop default that removes the paint-out entirely (superseding this branch's earlier in-repo attempt). Round 11 acted on this: it re-cropped and meshed r1 (and r2, and the non-clay gold) with the merged rectangular tool — see the r11 panel/round for the result.
