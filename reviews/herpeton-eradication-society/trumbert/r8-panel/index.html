Trumbert — Round 8 (layout-conditioning diagnostic)

📖 Round runbook →

R7 proved the late-slot (P3/P4) ghosting is positional and that flash31's cleanest sheets were exactly those where it ignored the supplied template. R8 tests how the layout is communicated across nine arms — from no template at all, through blank sizing canvases, pre-fill, per-angle, 1×2 pairs, ghost-panel regeneration, to a free deterministic normalization. All rolls flash31 @ 2K from the single R6 sword-shape base; restitching allowed (a recipe wins via assembly).

Headline: changing how layout is communicated removes the ghosting. No-template (A,B) and blank-canvas (C,D) came back clean — and blank-1×4 stayed clean even at the ghost-class 3512×1184 width, pinning the cause on the template's divider lines, not the wide aspect. Standard-order ghost-regeneration (H) repairs cleanly (2/3). 1×2 pairs (I) never ghost. Weak spots: pre-fill (E) drifts chibi and overwrites its front slot; per-angle (F) mirror-flips two of four views; gradual (G) breaks at the final slot.

Verdicts / info needed

The diagnosis is decisive; the calls are about the FORWARD PATH to a clean R9 Meshy input.

  1. Eye-verdict on the clean 4-view keeper-candidates: which recipe do you want to carry to R9 — no-template 1×4 (A1/A2), no-template 2×2 (B), blank-canvas (C/D), or the deterministic N-normalized sheet? All read clean; A2 and the blank-canvas rolls are the crispest.
  2. Build note: nearly every arm reads slightly sturdy / large-headed vs the lithe fey-gnome target (E is the worst, frankly chibi). Is this acceptable for the mini, or should R9 fold a lithe-build correction into the base before Meshy?
  3. Do you want the 1×2-pair assembly path (I) kept as a backup recipe? swordback-1 is a clean keeper; the front+shield pair needs the shield-side profile from frontshield-1 (frontshield-2 drifted its 2nd view to a rear).
  4. Confirm we DROP pre-fill (E), gradual (G), and per-angle (F) as production recipes — E/G are unreliable and F needs a chirality flip on 2/4 views; the no-template / blank-canvas / ghost-regen routes dominate them.
