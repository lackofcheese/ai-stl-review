Goal

The design is settled — this round exists only to lock Trumbert's pose before we commit to 3D. We render a spread of pose candidates on the two winning models so you can pick the single hero pose (and the model that renders it best). That pick becomes the concept keeper we clay-convert next round.

The choices on the table

Seven poses, all on the locked Kit-A fey design, each obeying the basing rule (both feet reach the ground) with both hands provable:

*(Lighter ideas held in reserve, easy to add: a one-knee Lay-on-Hands healer beat, or a relaxed "let's talk" diplomat stance.)*

What we need from you

  1. Pick the hero pose (P1–P7) — or a shortlist to take to two rolls each.
  2. Pick the model for that pose (gptimage2 vs riverflow25) once you see them, or let the best render decide.
  3. Approve the spend (~$3.60 for the full 7×2 board; a cheaper staged option below).

Then R4 = clay-convert the chosen pose+model keeper; R5 turnaround; R6 multi-view Meshy.

Cost

Full board ≈ $3.60: 7 poses × (gptimage2 $0.17 + riverflow25 $0.34) ≈ $0.51 each. Staged cheaper option ≈ $1.60: render all 7 on gptimage2 first (~$1.20), then the 2–3 pose finalists on riverflow25 (~$0.40) — you lose nothing on the pose read (pose is model-agnostic) and only pay riverflow's premium on the real contenders. Two rolls on the final pick when chosen.
