Gorgon — Round 11 (clay vs non-clay Meshy)

📖 Round runbook →

Round 11 re-runs Meshy with the hardened rectangular crop tool (main PR #80 — non-destructive, no mask paint-out, which fixes the r9/r10 dissolution) and tests one thing: does the clay conversion step earn its place? Three inputs, one Meshy run each — clay r1, clay r2, and the photoreal r8 champion with the clay step skipped entirely. All geometry-only (texturing off), scaled to 37 mm.

Pre-read. All three are clean single shells (0 holes, winding-consistent, only sub-mm slicer-repairable pinch edges). Headline: the non-clay (photoreal) mesh reconstructed as well as — arguably crisper than — the clay meshes, with the FEWEST non-manifold pinch edges (gold 10 · r2clay 18 · r1clay 30) and no invented base, background slab or texture-driven junk — the failure the clay step was meant to prevent did not appear (texturing is off, and the r8 sheet's background is a clean flat gray). The photoreal's real material/lighting cues seem to give Meshy slightly MORE to reconstruct. Fresh-context comparative verdict: the clay step is NOT needed for this character — non-clay shows no defect the clay meshes avoid, its hands are as good as (or better than) r2clay, and it's beaten on kit crispness only by r1clay. Crispness ranked r1clay > gold > r2clay, i.e. the sharpness spread is sheet-dependent, not a clay-vs-photoreal property. Gold's one real cost is cosmetic surface micro-noise. Medium confidence — a live-3D rotation should confirm the noise sands out at 37 mm and check for any finger-web / rear-boot sole-plate.
