Gorgon — Round 7

Round 7 in two parts. 7a generated four full turnaround sheets from the two Round 6 keeper bases (B2 plan-of-record + flash31 alt-hair), each through riverflow25 and flash31. 7b investigated why riverflow returned a cramped tall sheet, and adopts a 2×2 grid as the working default.

Decision — 2×2 is the de facto standard layout for now. Root cause: the output canvas aspect is inherited from an input reference. Gemini (flash31) matches the wide template; riverflow exposes no aspect_ratio param and copies the portrait character reference, so a 1×4 strip gets squeezed into tiny columns. A 2×2 grid matches that portrait shape and gives four large, legible views on both models.

Round 7a — turnaround generation (native layout)

The four sheets, all FIXABLE (no clean keeper, none reject). P2 — the non-pad-side ¾ view — failed on 3 of 4 (a facing-lock attractor). riverflow came back as a cramped tall 4-column strip (1664×2496), which motivated 7b. Click any sheet to open the gallery and arrow through them.
