Trumbert — Round 4 (design-to-survive thickening)

📖 Round runbook →

Pose and model are locked — Trumbert is the P6 Protective Interpose on gptimage2. This round does one job: make him printable. Four normally-fragile features are re-sculpted THICK/CHUNKY/SOLID for a Small-gnome resin mini — (1) one-mass flame hair (no thin wisps), (2) ears pinned/embedded in the hair, (3) a heavy thick FUR cloak, (4) a bulkier scimitar with its TIP PLANTED ON THE GROUND beside his foot (a gluable third contact). Two rendering approaches compete: Track A re-generates the interpose from a thickened text prompt; Track B img2img-edits the exact P6 keeper so identity/composition are preserved. Pick the best thickened base (or the best hybrid) to carry to the R5 clay-sculpt turnaround.

Pre-read — the thickening approach WORKS; the sword is the headline win. Fresh-context crop audits give three keeper-candidates: Track A gptimage2, and on Track B gptimage2 and riverflow25. Across the whole board, three of the four thickenings land reliably: the flame hair becomes one chunky connected mass, the cloak becomes a genuinely heavy deep-pile fur mantle (a big upgrade on the keeper's thin cape), and the blade bulks to a broad, print-safe cross-section — exactly the DM's most-agreed ask. The two harder targets are the ones to eye: (a) grounding the blade tip — several rolls (Track A riverflow25, Track B flash31/grokq) left the tip floating beside the boot instead of planted; the keepers plant it, though gptimage2/riverflow angle it out ~35° rather than dead-vertical; and (b) embedding the ears — pro2k, mai25 and Track A riverflow25 still poke a pointed tip out of the hair. Track identity trade-off: the img2img Track B best preserves the exact keeper face/composition (gptimage2 editing its own output), while the prompt-adjust Track A drifts the build chunkier/chibi.

Blind cross-check (read the chips — mandatory human look). The unbiased blind detector called every roll print-REJECT (its usual R1–R3 print-strict behaviour on a photoreal concept — it means "thicken more before 3D," not "discard"), and it flags two genuine crop-vs-blind disagreements for your eye: build — the blind pass reads several renders as "stout/stocky" where the primed crop audit sees "lithe/fey" (the soft R3 build tension, still live); and ears — on the two Track-B keepers the crop audit calls the ears embedded but the blind pass still sees a slightly-pointed tip. Also surfaced: the bulked blade now reads as a broad straightish falchion on some rolls rather than a clearly curved scimitar, and fine hardware (boot buckles, the ankh's neck chain) still reads thin — clay/mesh-stage items. Raw blind pass: notes/r4-blind-detect.jsonl.
