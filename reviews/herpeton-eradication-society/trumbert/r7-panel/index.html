Trumbert — Round 7 (turnaround ghosting investigation)

📖 Round runbook →

Diagnostic round: why do Trumbert's 1×4 turnarounds ghost in the late panels, and what fixes it? Five experiment axes — fresh controls with a new anti-ghosting prompt line (E0b/E0c), REVERSED view order (E1), one bounded 2×2 (E2), three divider treatments (E3–E5) — each run from BOTH bases, plus two flash31 whole-sheet self-repair prompts (F1/F2). Every sheet was screened by the calibrated turnaround-ghosting v2 detector, a fresh-context crop audit (with explicit sword-hand digit counts), and the blind detector.

Headline — ghosting is POSITIONAL, not content-driven. The reversed-order experiment (E1) is decisive: when the view order flips, the ghosting stays in canvas slots 3/4 — even though those slots now hold the front/shield views — while the back/sword views render crisp in slots 1/2. Late-slot spatial degradation (candidate cause #1) wins; view-content complexity loses.

What did NOT fix it: the anti-ghosting prompt line alone (E0c still ghosted 2/3 rolls, though its one clean roll is the round's best sheet); every divider treatment (E3–E5 all still ghost, and D2/D3 degrade regardless of grey/black/thin lines — divider damage looks like a co-symptom, not the cause); and both whole-sheet self-repairs (F1/F2 made P3 WORSE, leaked softness into clean P1/P2, and weakened all dividers — the generational-tax prediction confirmed; arm closed).

Base matters: the r6sword base ghosts harder, drifts chibi, inherits the thumb+3 hand everywhere, and broke the 4-view program on 3 sheets (missing/duplicated views). The r5gold base gave the round's three cleanest sheets. Also new: BOTH E1 roll-1 sheets broke the reversal at slot 3 the same way (a duplicate sword-side view) — slot-3 view-program fragility is its own failure mode.

Detector v2 live evaluation: its P3/P4 flags were overwhelmingly confirmed by the crop audits, but it produced 2 notable false positives on the E3-r6sword 'sculpted-toy'-style sheets (including the round's highest raw density, 0.457 — dense fur texture, actually crisp) and missed real ghosting on 3 sheets the crops caught (E2-r5gold boot film + hair dome; E5 P3 blade smears). Consistent with its calibrated 70% recall: a useful pre-filter, not a judge.

Blind-detect operational note: the first full blind pass was destroyed by a tooling bug (preflight banner polluting the JSON pipe — fixed in PR #154) after its paid calls ran; Dimitri authorized a full re-run over the original $2.06 cap. Round actuals $3.13 all-in (ledger-reconciled).
