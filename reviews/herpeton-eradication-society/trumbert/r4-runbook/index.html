Goal

The pose and model are settled — Trumbert is the Protective Interpose on gptimage2. This round does one job: make him printable. As a Small-gnome resin mini, the winning render has four fragile spots the DM says will snap or mush on a real print — thin flame-hair strands, pointy ears sticking out, a thin cloak/blade, and a scimitar tip floating in mid-air. We fix all four in the *concept image now* (pixels drive everything downstream, so it's cheapest to get thick geometry right before the turnaround and 3D steps), and we ground the blade tip so it becomes a gluable third contact point on the base.

The choices on the table

**One design, four thickenings — the decision is which *rendering approach* holds them best.** We run two parallel bets and you pick the best result (or the best hybrid):

The four fixesWhat changes
HairOne thick, chunky, connected mass of flame locks — no thin wisps or spikes
EarsShort, pinned back, embedded in the dense hair so no fragile pointed tips jut out
CloakA heavy thick FUR cloak (solid deep-pile volume, thick edges) — not a thin fabric cape
SwordA bulkier blade (wide cross-section) with its tip planted on the ground beside his foot — de-escalating *and* a third glue point

What we need from you

  1. Confirm the four fixes — especially two design calls: (a) the cloak becomes a thick fur cloak (your "maybe it's a thick fur cloak?"), and (b) the scimitar is lowered with its tip on the ground (rather than Blaize's alternative of resting it on the knee). Change any you'd rather not.

What changed going in (spec / prompt / process deltas)

  • No spec rewrite this round (the spec's print-safety section already demands chunky connected hair, thick blade cross-section, big geometric emblem). R4 is an *image-execution* pass that finally forces those existing design-to-survive rules into the pixels of the chosen keeper — the exact thin-hair-tips / thin-blade / thin-ankh items the R3 blind detector flagged. Any confirmed geometry changes (grounded blade, ears-in-hair, thick fur cloak) fold into spec.md *after* Dimitri signs off on the winning base, not before.
  • New prompts:
    • prompts/r4-interpose-thick.txt — Track A. The R3 interpose prompt rebuilt around "built to be 3D-printed… everything THICK, CHUNKY, SOLID": one-mass hair (no wisps), ears pinned/embedded in hair, heavy thick fur cloak, bulky grounded blade (tip on the floor, third contact), plain chunky pommel.
    • prompts/r4-interpose-thicken-i2i.txt — Track B. A tight edit instruction: KEEP identity/kit/pose/chirality/composition, change ONLY the four features to thicker/solid, and re-ground the blade tip.
  • Model policy (img2img): flash31 is the standard img2img performer and the reliability anchor; grokq + mai25 ride along as the active cheap A/B experiment (README image-model policy). gptimage2 + riverflow25 are added to the img2img set at Dimitri's request (gptimage2 made the keeper, so editing its own output may preserve identity best), **with the known caveat that gptimage2/seedream tend to *re-compose* edit sheets** — flash31 stays the trust anchor if an edit drifts. pro2k is the Gemini backup.
  • Process: this is its own numbered round (runbook-before-spend). Blaize's finger-merge caveat is logged but out of scope — it applies to the P2 open-palm pose, not P6 (both hands grip). Grounding the blade follows the skill's basing rule (both feet + a grounded blade = a third contact point; cf. P5 dawn-salute's grounded-blade trick) and Blaize's "support the sword" suggestion.
