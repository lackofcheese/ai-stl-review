Goal

Two things at once, from the same pinned design (spec.md):

  • Track A — get a look at Trumbert as an appealing photoreal hero image so Dimitri can sign off on the DESIGN before we spend iterations on turnarounds, clay, or a multi-view Meshy run. The "does this look like my character?" gate.
  • Track B — a pathway-value baseline: feed the description STRAIGHT to Meshy (text-to-3D, no concept art, no turnaround) and take the raw mesh. This is the control we'll later hold the full pipeline's mesh up against, to decide whether the concept-art + turnaround detour actually earns its cost — for Trumbert and for the rest of the party.

The design itself is already pinned; this round renders it (A) and reconstructs it the cheap way (B) so both the design and the pathway question move forward together.

The choices on the table

Track A — one pinned design rendered by ~10 image models from the same prompt; you pick which render best captures Trumbert (burly little gnome, warm redeemer's face, raised sun-etched scimitar + sunburst shield, both feet planted) — or reject all with notes.

Track B — no design choice; one approach (direct text→Meshy, two rolls, keep the cleaner). What it sets up is the pathway-value read:

The questionHow this round feeds it
Which image model best *is* Trumbert?Track A concept panel — your keeper pick
Does text-to-3D capture him at all?Track B baseline mesh — build, gear, sunburst, weapon/shield, feet
What does the concept-art + turnaround detour buy?Track B is the "before"; the pipeline-path mesh (later rounds) is the "after", compared side by side

Expected Track-B weaknesses to watch (text-to-3D's known trade-off vs multi-view image-to-3D): pose/handedness drift, softer sunburst/ankh, thin-part fragility on the raised scimitar — exactly what multi-view is supposed to fix.

What we need from you

  1. Sign off on the spec (trumbert/spec.md) — or redline anything.
  2. Approve the spend: Track A ~$1 (image sweep) and Track B Meshy credits (two text-to-3D rolls). Approve either/both.
  3. At round close: pick a Track-A design keeper (or reject-all with notes), and later give the qualitative "is the detour worth it?" read once a pipeline-path mesh exists.

Cost

  • Track B: Meshy credits, not USD — ~5–20 credits per text-to-3D preview roll × 2. Verify the credit balance/billing on first use (API pool may differ from the web-subscription pool).
Technical plan — models, prompts, commands (pipeline detail)

What changed going in (spec / prompt / tooling / process deltas)

  • New project + subject (fantasy RPG). Spec written fresh (trumbert/spec.md) with explicit chirality anchors (scimitar = his RIGHT hand / viewer's left; shield = his LEFT arm) — everything else centered → mirror-cheap.
  • Design-to-survive baked into both prompts: Sarenrae sunburst (tabard + shield boss) and ankh specified BIG, geometric, high-relief; thick scimitar blade; chunky hair locks; both feet planted for basing.
  • Two prompts, two media: Track A prompts/r1-front-photoreal.txt (photoreal studio render for the image sweep); Track B prompts/r1-meshy-text.txt (written for a 3D figure — "a single 3D tabletop miniature figurine of…", solid connected forms, no base, single character).
  • Tooling: tools/meshy_api.py gains a run-text mode (text-to-3D v2 preview, geometry-only, raw/no-remesh — same raw default as the image path that avoided the gorgon r10/r11 roughness regression). status/download take --kind image|text. Image-to-3D path unchanged.
  • Process note (intentional, logged): the skill's letter makes each Meshy run its own numbered round; here the direct-text Meshy baseline is folded into Round 1 as Track B of one deliberate pathway-value A/B (nothing run yet, so no post-hoc-appendage concern — the plan-before-spend gate is met). The LATER multi-view Meshy run remains its own numbered round, and is compared back to this Track-B baseline.
