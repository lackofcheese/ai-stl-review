Trumbert — Round 6 (sword-prompting A/B/C/D + faithful turnaround)

📖 Round runbook →

Two concurrent tracks. Track A is a small A/B/C/D experiment: after the R4 broadsword and the R5 kukri, we tested the sword-prompting techniques separately to see which actually gets a curved scimitar — a plain baseline vs analogy vs "convex" vs a pinned curve-direction, each on flash31 + gptimage2, 2 rolls (16 images). Track B is a faithful full-colour turnaround (flash31 1×4, 3 rolls) of the R5 gold render — the multi-view input for the 3D step.

Track A headline — the technique barely mattered; the word "re-curve" was the whole problem. All 16/16 images came back a correct single convex scimitar with a correct thumb-plus-four-finger grip — including the plain baseline with no special wording. No broadswords, no kukris, no mirror-flips. Dropping "re-curve" (which R5's flash31 read as recurve) fixed it; analogy / "convex" / direction-pinning added no measurable reliability over the baseline. The real split is by MODEL: flash31 = broad, print-sturdy falchion blade but a slightly stocky build and a floaty tip; gptimage2 = slim elegant saber + a lithe/fey build and better grounding, but a thin, fragile tip. The unbiased blind detector independently confirmed the blade is curved on all 16 (no straight/kukri), and — as in R5 — flags build ("not lithe enough") across the board: that build read is the one thing worth your eye. (Its "wrong-arm" chirality flags are a known blind L/R-confusion artifact — the config matches the gold.)

Track B — all three turnarounds PASS (true 4-view turntable, true back with no face, identity + colour faithful, curved scimitar consistent, correct grips). Ranking -3 > -1 > -2. Universal flags: a protruding pointed ear + a thin blade tip (print-fragility), and the shield-over-face occlusion inherent to the shield-side profile.
